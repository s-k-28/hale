HALE

Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 12, 2026

HALE is a nicotine-cessation support app for adults 21 and older. This page explains what we store, why we store it, who processes it, and how to delete it. The short version: we store what the app needs to work, we show no ads, we sell no data, and we do no cross-app tracking.

What we collect

  • Account. Accounts are anonymous by default: a random account ID, and optionally a first name. An email address is stored only if you choose to link one.
  • Your quit profile and progress. The product you're quitting, how much you used and what it cost (which we use only to calculate your savings), your triggers and toughest hour, your motivation, daily check-ins, logged cravings (time, intensity, trigger, context), and relapse history. This is self-reported, health-related information; it exists so the app can show your progress, and it is never used for advertising.
  • Coach conversations. Messages you send to Sage, the in-app coach, and its replies.
  • Buddies, squads, and referrals. Who you're paired with, squads you join, and referral records (who invited whom) so accountability features and invite rewards work. Buddies see only your first name, streak, and last check-in date — never your cravings, messages, or money figures.
  • Purchases. Your subscription status. Payment itself is handled by Apple; we never see your card details.
  • Device signals. A push-notification token if you allow notifications, your timezone (so streaks roll over on your day), and app-usage analytics events. If crash reporting is enabled, crash diagnostics.

Who processes it for us

HALE runs on a small set of service providers, each receiving only what its job needs:

  • Convex — our database; stores the account and content above.
  • Anthropic — when you message Sage, your message and recent conversation context are sent to Anthropic's API to generate the reply. Sage offers general support, not medical advice.
  • RevenueCat — subscription status and purchase events.
  • OneSignal — delivers push notifications you've opted into.
  • PostHog — product analytics (which features are used), keyed to your account ID.
  • Sentry — crash reports, if enabled in the build.

No tracking. HALE contains no advertising SDKs, does not use the IDFA, and does not track you across other companies' apps or websites.

Deleting your data

You can permanently delete your account and its content at any time, in the app: You tab → Delete account. This erases your quit history, check-ins, cravings, coach conversations, goals, squads you own, and your buddy pairing, and it cannot be undone. Referral records that already counted toward another person's reward are kept, without your profile attached.

Deleting your account does not cancel an App Store subscription — Apple owns that billing relationship. Manage it in Settings → Apple ID → Subscriptions.

Age

HALE is for adults 21 and older. We do not knowingly collect data from anyone under 21.

Not medical advice

HALE is a self-help support tool, not a medical device, and provides no medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Recovery timelines shown in the app are cited in the app under You → Disclaimers & sources.
